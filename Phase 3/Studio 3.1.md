Visual Prompt 1: Create a stone sculpture of an Egyptian king sitting on a throne. The figure should be facing forward in a stiff, upright position. The body should look strong and balanced, with both sides being symmetrical. The face should look calm and serious, not showing much emotion.

Behind the king’s head, include a falcon spreading its wings to show protection. The sculpture should look smooth, solid, and long-lasting.

This artwork shows how Egyptians believed their rulers were connected to the gods. The purpose of the sculpture was to show power and remind people that the king had divine authority. It was also made to last forever so the king could live on in the afterlife.

Visual Prompt 2: Create a painting that shows people inside a building using perspective to make it look realistic. The space should look deep, with lines going toward one point in the background. The figures should look like real humans with natural body shapes and soft shadows.

Use light and shadow to make the figures look more three-dimensional. The colors should be balanced and not too bright.

This artwork shows how artists in the Renaissance started focusing more on realism and science. They used math and observation to make their art look more real. The purpose was not only religious, but also to show knowledge and a better understanding of the world.
